About Me

I am currently pursuing a double Master’s degree in Pure and Applied Mathematics, split between TUM and EPFL. My academic journey began at TUM, where I earned two Bachelor’s degrees in Engineering Science and Mathematics.

My studies and independent research are driven by a fascination with the structures of both the physical world and abstract ideas, specifically through the lenses of stochastic analysis, dynamical systems, and mathematical physics. Examples of my interests include: Minimum Gap Theory on Metric Spaces, Apollonian Packing Spaces, Random Perturbations of Graph-Valued Stochastic Processes, Neural Stochastic Differential Equations, etc.

Outside of my research, I stay active through jogging, biking, and swimming. I find a similar sense of rhythm and structure in music as I do in mathematics; I am a passionate pianist and composer. You can listen to some of my original compositions on Spotify (Musical Archives).
