Brief read - Measure theory, optimization, machine learning

This note explains how moving probability mass can be used to study optimization paths. The key idea is to compare distributions through transport maps rather than pointwise errors.

In practice, this viewpoint helps interpret convergence behavior in stochastic systems and clarifies why some dynamics remain stable under perturbations.

\[ W_2^2(\mu,\nu) = \inf_{\gamma \in \Pi(\mu,\nu)} \int_{\mathbb{R}^d \times \mathbb{R}^d} \|x-y\|^2 \, d\gamma(x,y) \]

Impact: gives a geometric language for model behavior and bridges probability, PDE, and optimization.

Brief read - Probability, random structures, data science

This note summarizes why high-dimensional random objects often stay close to their expected value. The result is a practical toolkit for proving reliability of randomized algorithms.

The main lesson is that tail bounds become a design principle: they translate uncertainty into explicit confidence guarantees.

\[ \mathbb{P}\!\left(|X - \mathbb{E}X| \ge t\right) \le 2\exp\!\left(-\frac{2t^2}{\sum_{i=1}^n c_i^2}\right) \]

Impact: supports robust reasoning in modern AI pipelines where randomness is unavoidable.
